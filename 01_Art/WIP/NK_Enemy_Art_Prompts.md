# Notebook Knight — Enemy Art Prompt Sheet (non-boss)

42 regular enemies (bosses excluded, as requested). Every non-boss enemy can appear as an **Inked elite** (first draft) and a **Colored elite** (Second Draft/NG+), so each gets three prompts: **Base / Inked / Colored**.

## Style bible (read first)

- **Base art** is graphite pencil doodle — mostly monochrome with at most a whisper of colored pencil. This matters: the game currently styles elites automatically with canvas filters (Inked = darkened/high-contrast, Colored = hue-saturated), so light, low-saturation base art keeps those filters readable **until** dedicated elite art lands. Once you have Inked/Colored art, I can wire the sprite registry to prefer per-variant files.
- **Deliverables:** transparent-background PNG, ~800×1000 px (drawn area fills the frame; in-game footprint is ~160×200). One character, no ground shadow, no text.
- **File naming:** `enemy_<id>.png`, `enemy_<id>_inked.png`, `enemy_<id>_colored.png` (ids below).
- **Consistency trick:** generate the Base first, then feed it back as an image reference with the Inked/Colored prompt so the silhouette stays identical.
- **Shared sketches today:** several enemies currently reuse one procedural sketch (all slimes, both bats, three goblins, two skeletons, three spiders, two orcs, ghost/shade-walker/bog-wraith, wolf/cinder-hound, wisp/frost-acolyte/void-wraith, harpy/sky-harrier, gargoyle/clockwork-sentry, cultist/cult-priest, dark-knight/legionnaire/throne-guardian, four mimic tiers). The prompts below give each enemy a **distinct** design — drawing them all upgrades enemy variety enormously, but if you want to economize, families are grouped adjacently so you can draw one per family first.
- The four Mimic tiers share one design (they differ only in stats); one art set covers all four.

### Slime  
`slime` — Meadow Path

**Base:** Hand-drawn pencil sketch of a round, wobbly blob of jelly with two big cartoon eyes and a worried little smile, a couple of goo drips sliding off its sides, sitting in a small puddle of itself. Drawn like a kid's doodle in a school notebook: rough graphite linework, wobbly but confident strokes, light crosshatch shading, the faintest wash of green colored pencil. Full body, single character, centered, facing slightly left, plain white background, no text, no border, child-friendly storybook menace. Tall portrait format (4:5), sized to read clearly at 160x200 px.

**Inked elite:** The same Slime from Notebook Knight, re-drawn entirely in bold black INK: heavy confident pen strokes, solid black fills in the shadows, thicker silhouette, a few ink-splatter flecks, posture 20% more menacing. Same notebook-doodle character design, full body, plain white background, no text. This is the 'Inked' elite variant - it must read as the exact same creature traced over in permanent marker.

**Colored elite:** The same Slime from Notebook Knight, aggressively colored with saturated marker and crayon strokes that sometimes scribble outside the lines - lime green and swampy teal marker, goo drips in neon green. Keep the bold ink outlines visible underneath. Same notebook-doodle character design, full body, plain white background, no text. This is the 'Colored' elite variant (Second Draft/NG+) - like a kid attacked the drawing with every marker in the box.

## Production order suggestion

1. **The Eraser** — unique nemesis, biggest wow-per-image, currently a procedural smudge.
2. **One per family the player meets first:** Slime, Goblin, Wolf, Skeleton, Bat, Spider, Zombie, Bandit — covers ~80% of early-game encounters.
3. **Mid-game distinct silhouettes:** Ghost, Cultist, Orc, Lizardman, Gargoyle, Harpy, Fire Imp, Ice Wisp, Necromancer, Dark Knight, Mimic.
4. **Late-game (players are invested by now):** Shade Walker, Bog Wraith, Iron Legionnaire, Frost Acolyte, Sky Harrier, Chasm Stalker, Cinder Hound, Clockwork Sentry, Void Wraith, Throne Guardian.
5. **Inked variants** in the same order; **Colored variants** last (NG+ audience only).

Full art count: 39 distinct designs cover all 42 enemies (the four Mimic tiers share one). 39 base + 38 inked + 38 colored (the Eraser never rolls elite, so it needs no variants) = **115 images**, or roughly half that if families share art initially.
