Ready-to-paste prompts for the 15 remaining chest pieces (Quilted Vest already has art), split

## Batch 1 — Common/Uncommon basics

Leather Armor, Copper Breastplate, Bronze Breastplate, Chainmail, Iron Plate.

```
Here is the reference character (attached). I need 5 versions of them, arranged in a clean grid
on one sheet, each in the exact same three-quarter pose facing slightly left, arms hanging
naturally at the sides, same head/hair/face/proportions - only the chest/torso covering (and
matching lower-leg armor, see below) changes between panels.

ART STYLE - match the reference exactly, this is the most important instruction: rough,
hand-drawn pencil-and-ink sketch linework, visible pencil texture and cross-hatching for
shading, like a page torn from a sketchbook. This is a FLAT LINE DRAWING, not a painted or
rendered illustration - do not add smooth painterly shading, soft airbrush gradients, dramatic
rim lighting, glow effects, or a dark vignette background. The background must be a single flat
plain light color (light gray or white, no gradient, no vignette, no glow) exactly like the
reference image's plain background - nothing atmospheric.

Each chest piece covers the torso down to roughly hip/upper-thigh like the reference's own
tunic, since it's the only visible torso garment and needs to read as complete on its own.
Additionally, since these are meant to look like full matching armor sets, extend coverage down
onto the lower legs (shin/greave area) in the same material, rather than leaving the legs bare
under the tunic like the base reference - the legs should visibly look armored/protected too,
not just the torso.

Panel 1: Leather Armor - simple tanned hide cuirass, visibly stitched-together leather panels,
laced sides, humble and practical, no metal. Include matching leather leg wraps/wrappings over
the shins.

Panel 2: Copper Breastplate - a basic copper-toned metal chest plate, simple rounded shape,
plain leather shoulder straps, minimal ornamentation, dull warm metal sheen - entry-level armor.
Include simple matching copper shin guards.

Panel 3: Bronze Breastplate - a polished bronze cuirass with a classic soldier's-armor shape,
slightly more defined contouring than the copper piece, leather straps at the shoulders, a
practical military look. Include matching bronze greaves over the shins.

Panel 4: Chainmail - a mail hauberk made of hundreds of tiny interlocking rings (draw as a dense
fine circular/scale texture, not solid metal), hip-length, a plain cloth collar/cuff peeking out
underneath. Continue the mail texture down as leggings visible below the hem, rather than bare
legs.

Panel 5: Iron Plate - a heavier, bulkier solid iron chest plate, thicker and more armored-looking
than the bronze piece, riveted seams, duller gray-iron tone, conveys real weight and coverage.
Include matching solid iron greaves over the shins.

Keep every panel's pose, camera framing, and character scale identical so they can be cut apart
and compared directly - no panel zoomed, rotated, or scaled differently. Plain flat light
background per panel as described above, clean gaps between panels so each character can be
cropped out cleanly.
```
